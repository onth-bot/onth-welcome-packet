Call-Out Policy
Overview

Reliable attendance is an essential function of your job. In rare instances when an employee cannot avoid being late, is unable to work as scheduled, or must leave early, they must personally notify their supervisor as soon as possible — and always in advance of the anticipated absence.

Attendance is tracked on a 6-week rolling cycle with a 3-point limit. Reaching a 4th point triggers an automatic Final Write-Up and Attendance Probation.

Call-In Procedures
  • Always call the Dispatch Line (559.753.1850) as soon as you know you cannot make your shift.
  • If you can't reach a supervisor, leave a voicemail and keep following up until you speak with someone directly.
  • Failure to follow proper call-in procedures may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
  • Missing 2 scheduled shifts without any notice is considered Job Abandonment and will be treated as a voluntary resignation.
Probation & Excused Absences
  • Reaching 3 points within a 6-week cycle results in a final write-up and a 1-month perfect-attendance probation period.
  • Surpassing 3 points or violating attendance probation is subject to termination.
A point is excused by using a full 8 hours of PTO, submitting a doctor's note, or providing a police report for the day missed.

Rest Period Rules
  • Employees receive one 15-minute paid rest break for every 4 hours worked. For an 8-hour shift that means two 15-minute breaks.
  • Breaks should be taken roughly in the middle of each work period. It is your responsibility to take them on time — don't wait to be reminded.
  • Rest periods are paid and counted as hours worked. You do not need to log them on your timecard.
  • Rest breaks cannot be combined with each other or with your meal period.
  • Rest breaks cannot be waived to shorten your workday or saved up for any other purpose.
Not allowed: Taking both 15-min breaks back-to-back, combining them with your 30-min lunch, taking them before you clock on, or using them to clock out early.
Meal Break Violation

Your 30-minute lunch must be completed before your 5th hour of work — at minimum 1 minute before the 5-hour mark. Failure results in a Meal Break Violation and an automatic Final Write-Up. Two or more violations may lead to termination.

Route Expectations & SPH
Expectation

ONTH requires all Delivery Associates to complete their assigned routes within the standard time set by Amazon. Because every route is different, your main goal is to stay "On Time" or "Ahead" of the Amazon routing curve throughout your shift.

To keep a good pace, stay organized in the van, walk briskly to each delivery location, and minimize distractions. Safety is always the top priority — never rush or compromise safety to work faster.

Falling 15–20 stops behind the Amazon standard means you will need a rescue. Consistently needing rescues to finish a standard route is a failure to meet job expectations and may result in disciplinary action.
Recommended SPH Benchmarks

Every route is different — your exact required pace will vary. Use these as your target ranges.

Safety Infraction Policy
Policy Statement

Safe driving is a fundamental requirement of your position. The safety of our team and the public is our highest priority and is strictly non-negotiable. All safety infractions are closely monitored, officially documented, and will result in immediate disciplinary action.

Disciplinary Procedures
  • Immediate Action: Your route will be paused immediately (dependent on rescue availability) and you will receive a formal written warning.
  • Alternate Action: If operational constraints prevent a same-day route pause, you will forfeit your next scheduled shift in lieu of the route pause.
Standard Infractions

Results in a same-day route pause (or forfeited shift) + formal write-up.

  • Seat Belt Violation
  • Following Distance Violation
  • Distracted Driving
  • Stop Sign Violation
  • Illegal U-Turn
  • Speeding
Severe Infractions

Demonstrates a critical lapse in safety. Results in a route pause + immediate suspension or termination.

  • Red Light Infraction — Severe
  • Stop Sign Infraction — Severe
  • Speeding — Severe

Policy
Accidents & Vehicle Damage
What You Need to Know

You are on the road all day, and we understand that minor things can happen. When they do, communication is everything. How you handle a situation matters far more than the situation itself — honesty and quick reporting will always work in your favor.

Minor Incidents — Always Report It
Fender bender, scrape, or any contact with an object or vehicle.

If anything happens — no matter how small — call Dispatch immediately. Do not wait until you return to the station. As long as you are honest and upfront with us, we will work through it together. A quick phone call goes a long way. It is the cover-ups and delays that cause the real problems, not the incident itself.

Major Incidents — Formal Review
Gross negligence, reckless driving, or totaling a vehicle.

Severe incidents are handled differently and will require a formal safety and employment review. These situations are taken very seriously and may result in suspension or termination depending on the circumstances. Safe, responsible driving is a non-negotiable part of this job.

Policy
The Rescue Policy
We Operate as a Team

At ONTH, we look out for one another. The goal at the end of every shift is simple: everyone gets back to the station safely and on time. That only happens when the team works together.

Helping Out — Doing a Rescue
When you finish early and a teammate needs help.

If you finish your route early, Dispatch may ask you to pick up a few stops from a teammate who is running behind. Refusing a rescue is not acceptable. We are a team — today you're helping out, and tomorrow you might be the one who needs it. A positive attitude toward rescues reflects well on your performance and your character.

Needing Help — Being Rescued
When you're running behind and need stops taken.

We are always willing to help on a tough day — heavy routes and unexpected delays happen to everyone. However, consistently needing to be rescued shift after shift signals a performance issue. If this becomes a pattern, we will schedule a review to work with you on improving your pace and routing efficiency. The goal is to set you up for success, not to penalize you.
